Child Support Reduction

Guidance on modifying German child support (Unterhalt) obligations where your financial circumstances have changed, from a UK-based German legal expert.

German Child Support Law

In Germany, child support (Unterhalt) is governed by the Bürgerliches Gesetzbuch (BGB) and calculated using the Düsseldorf Table (Düsseldorfer Tabelle), which sets out standard maintenance amounts based on the payer's income and the child's age. If your financial circumstances have changed since a child support order was made, you may be able to apply to have the amount reduced.

Changes such as a reduction in income, loss of employment, additional maintenance obligations, or significant changes in the child's living arrangements may all provide grounds for modification.

When Modification May Be Possible

  • Your income has fallen significantly since the original order was made
  • You have lost employment or your working hours have been reduced
  • You have taken on significant new financial obligations, such as supporting another child
  • The child has become financially independent
  • The child's living arrangements have changed and they now spend more time with you
  • Your existing Unterhalt payments exceed the limits permitted by the self-sufficiency threshold (Selbstbehalt)

How We Can Help

Your consultation (£100 fixed fee) covers a review of your current obligations and circumstances. We will explain the German Unterhalt rules that apply to your situation, assess whether a modification application is likely to succeed, and advise on the steps required.

  • Reviewing your current child support obligation and the basis on which it was set
  • Assessing whether changed circumstances meet the threshold for modification under German law
  • Advising on the formal modification process and what evidence is required
  • Explaining the Selbstbehalt rules that protect a minimum income for the paying parent
  • Guidance on negotiating an agreed variation with the other party
