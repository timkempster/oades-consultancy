German Pension Claims

If you worked in Germany, you may have built up valuable pension entitlements. We help UK and international residents trace, understand, and claim what they are owed from the German state pension system.

German State Pension Entitlements

Anyone who has worked in Germany and paid into the German statutory pension insurance scheme (gesetzliche Rentenversicherung) builds up pension entitlements – regardless of whether they are a German citizen or later moved abroad. These contributions do not disappear when you leave Germany; they remain credited to your account with the Deutsche Rentenversicherung (DRV) and can be claimed when you reach pensionable age.

Many people who worked in Germany for a period are unaware of the entitlements they have accumulated, or are unsure how to access them from abroad. Some have contributions on record that they cannot recall or trace. We help clients understand and navigate the claims process.

Who Is Entitled to Claim

  • UK residents who worked in Germany at any point in their career
  • People who paid German Rentenversicherung contributions and have since left Germany
  • Beneficiaries of a deceased person who may have had unclaimed German pension entitlements
  • Individuals who have received a letter from the Deutsche Rentenversicherung they do not understand
  • People approaching pension age who want to check whether they have German entitlements

  • Advising on how to obtain your German pension account statement (Kontoklärung)

Pension Contributions Made Decades Ago

German pension records stretch back many decades and contributions are not lost simply because time has passed or because you have moved abroad. Even if you only worked in Germany briefly, contributions made during that period remain on your record. It is always worth checking.

UK–Germany Social Security Agreement: The UK and Germany have a social security agreement which affects how contribution periods are totalled for pension eligibility purposes. We can advise on how this agreement applies to your specific situation.
