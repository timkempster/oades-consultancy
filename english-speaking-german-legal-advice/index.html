Specialist German Legal Advice for English Speakers

Dealing with a German legal matter is challenging enough without also navigating a different legal system in a foreign language. Oades Consultancy provides expert German legal guidance delivered entirely in English, making specialist advice accessible to clients in the UK, the United States, Australia, Canada, and anywhere else in the world.

Whether you are facing an employment dispute with a German employer, dealing with a tenancy issue in Germany, or need help understanding your rights under a German contract, we provide clear and actionable advice at a fixed fee.

Areas We Cover

  • Employment law – wrongful dismissal, unpaid wages, notice periods, redundancy, and workplace disputes under German law
  • Tenancy disputes – rental agreements, deposit recovery, landlord obligations, and tenancy termination under German law
  • Visa and residence – understanding German residence permits, permanent residence (Niederlassungserlaubnis), and documentation requirements
  • Contract and business law – reviewing German contracts, understanding obligations, and resolving commercial disputes
  • Social security matters – understanding German social-welfare entitlements and contribution histories
  • Consumer rights – refunds, warranties, and consumer protections under German law

How a Consultation Works

Your one-hour consultation (£100 fixed fee) takes place online via video or telephone. Before the session, you are welcome to email any relevant documents – contracts, letters, correspondence – so that we can review them in advance and make the best use of your consultation time.

By the end of the session you will have a clear understanding of your legal position, the options available to you, and the realistic steps you can take to resolve your matter. Where follow-on work is required, we provide a written quote before proceeding.

Please note: Oades Consultancy is not admitted as a Rechtsanwalt in Germany. We provide consultancy advice from the UK and do not represent clients in German courts or formal proceedings.
