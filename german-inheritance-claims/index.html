German Inheritance Claims

Guidance for UK-based and international beneficiaries seeking to assert their rights in a German estate – including forced heirship and contested inheritances.

Claiming Your Inheritance from a German Estate

If a family member has died leaving an estate in Germany and you believe you are entitled to a share, German law provides clear mechanisms for asserting that entitlement – even if you were excluded from the will or are based outside Germany.

German inheritance law (Erbrecht) is notably different from UK probate law, particularly in its treatment of forced heirship and the rights of close family members. Understanding your position requires specialist knowledge of German law.

Forced Heirship (Pflichtteil)

One of the most important features of German inheritance law is the Pflichtteil – a statutory right to a minimum share of an estate for close family members (children, spouses, and in some cases parents), even if they have been excluded from the will. This right cannot be overridden by a testator, and it applies regardless of where in the world the beneficiary lives.

If you have been cut out of a German will but are a close relative of the deceased, you may still have a legally enforceable claim to a portion of the estate.

How We Can Help

  • Assessing your potential entitlement under German inheritance law
  • Explaining the Pflichtteil rules and whether they apply to your situation
  • Advising on time limits – claims must generally be asserted within three years of learning of the inheritance and the exclusion from the will
  • Guidance on obtaining documentation to establish your entitlement
  • Advising on the German Nachlassgericht (probate court) process
  • Help understanding and responding to communications from German estate administrators or notaries

Contested Estates

Where there is a dispute over the validity of a will, the identity of heirs, or the value of estate assets, the matter may need to be resolved through the German court system. We can explain the process and advise on the merits of your position before you commit to formal proceedings.

Please note: Oades Consultancy is not admitted as a Rechtsanwalt in Germany and cannot represent you in German court proceedings. Where formal legal representation is required, we can advise on the process and assist you in engaging appropriate local representation.
