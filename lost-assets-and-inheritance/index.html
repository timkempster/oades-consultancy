Lost Assets & Inheritance

Tracing lost German assets, guiding you through German inheritance proceedings, and helping you recover funds you may not have known existed.

Recovering Lost German Assets

Many people have unclaimed assets in Germany – dormant bank accounts, unclaimed insurance policies, forgotten savings, or investments they were unaware of. These assets may belong to you directly or form part of a German estate you stand to inherit. Without knowing where to look or how German institutions work, recovering them can seem impossible from abroad.

Oades Consultancy provides practical guidance on how to trace and recover lost German assets, using the legitimate channels available under German law.

German Inheritance Law

German inheritance law (Erbrecht) differs significantly from UK probate law. Germany operates a system of universal succession, meaning heirs inherit both the assets and the liabilities of an estate simultaneously. There are strict time limits on accepting or disclaiming an inheritance, and the process of establishing entitlement can be complex – particularly for beneficiaries living outside Germany.

We help clients understand their rights and obligations under German inheritance law, navigate the probate process, and take the appropriate steps to claim what they are owed.

How We Can Help

  • Advising on your rights as an heir under German law, including forced heirship (Pflichtteil) provisions
  • Guidance on the German probate process and the role of the Nachlassgericht (probate court)
  • Assistance tracing lost bank accounts, insurance policies, and other unclaimed financial assets in Germany
  • Advising on time limits for accepting or disclaiming a German inheritance
  • Help understanding German inheritance tax (Erbschaftsteuer) implications for non-residents
  • Guidance on obtaining a German Certificate of Inheritance (Erbschein)

Asset Tracing

Germany has established registries and procedures for tracing unclaimed financial assets. We can advise you on which organisations to contact, what documentation you need, and how to pursue your claim through the appropriate channels.

Please note: Oades Consultancy is not admitted as a Rechtsanwalt in Germany. Where formal legal representation before German courts or registries is required, we can advise on engaging appropriate local representation.
